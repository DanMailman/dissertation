To my family, for being my first teachers and my safest harbor.

To my parents, who taught me that curiosity is a form of love: for books, for people, for the world. Your steady faith in the long game made it possible to keep going when progress was measured in paragraphs.

To my partner, whose patience turned late nights into livable nights, whose laughter kept this work human, and whose quiet “you’ve got this” carried more weight than any footnote. Thank you for listening to half-formed ideas until they became sentences.

To my mentors and friends, who challenged me, pushed when I stalled, and let up when I needed air; your generosity is stitched into every chapter.

And to those I miss, whose names I carry into every room: your memory has been a compass. This dissertation is, in part, my attempt to honor what you planted—discipline, tenderness, and the stubborn hope that hard work can become a gift.

This work is dedicated to you.
